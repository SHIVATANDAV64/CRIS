## Core Mechanism
SafeCtrlBO combines safe Bayesian optimization with additive Gaussian process (GP) kernels for tuning multiple coupled controllers. The method uses **additive GP kernels** that decompose the high-dimensional controller parameter space into lower-dimensional components, encoding the insight that controller gains often affect performance in partially separable ways. This reduces sample complexity compared to full-dimensional kernels. Additionally, it replaces the computationally expensive "potential-expander" safety exploration mechanism used in SafeOpt-style methods with a **boundary-based expansion rule** that identifies safe regions by examining the geometric constraints at the boundary of the current safe set, dramatically reducing computational overhead while preserving high-probability safety guarantees.

## Key Insight
The key insight is two-fold: (1) Controller parameters often exhibit low-order interaction effects rather than complex coupling across all dimensions simultaneously, making additive structure a reasonable and efficient assumption. (2) The expensive potential-expander computation can be replaced by a simpler boundary examination that achieves equivalent safe-set expansion behavior under explicit geometric conditions, trading computational complexity for practical deployability.

## Domain-Blind Abstraction
This is a constrained optimization method that uses probabilistic models to explore a parameter space while maintaining safety constraints. It improves efficiency by assuming the objective function has additive structure (can be decomposed into lower-dimensional components) and replaces expensive constraint-checking computations with a more efficient geometric approach. The method maintains probabilistic guarantees that explored parameters will not cause system failure.
