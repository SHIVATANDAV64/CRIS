## Core Mechanism
The paper develops a generative model by minimizing the second-order Wasserstein loss ($W_2$) through a distribution-dependent ordinary differential equation (ODE). The ODE dynamics involve the **Kantorovich potential** — a dual formulation of optimal transport — associated with both the true data distribution and the current model estimate. The key mathematical construction shows that the time-marginal laws of this ODE form a **gradient flow** for the $W_2$ loss, meaning the distribution evolves "downhill" toward the target in the Wasserstein space. An Euler discretization scheme is proposed that recovers this gradient flow in the infinite-step limit. The algorithm incorporates **persistent training**, which maintains multiple model versions during optimization to better track the gradient flow trajectory.

## Key Insight
The paper establishes that the $W_2$ loss admits a gradient flow structure in the space of probability measures equipped with the Wasserstein-2 geometry. This gradient flow converges **exponentially fast** to the true data distribution under certain conditions. The insight is that by formulating the generative process as following this gradient flow — rather than directly minimizing a neural network loss — one obtains a theoretically grounded optimization dynamics. The persistent training mechanism naturally aligns with this approach by allowing the model to "flow" toward the target distribution rather than making aggressive updates.
