## Core Mechanism
STELLA is a multimodal large language model that synergistically combines three modalities: **protein sequence** (primary amino acid chain), **protein structure** (tertiary 3D conformation), and **natural language** (functional descriptions). The system uses ESM3 as a unified bimodal encoder that generates joint representations from both sequence and structural inputs, then aligns these representations with textual embeddings from Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct. This alignment enables cross-modality reasoning—given a protein's sequence and structure, the model can generate functional descriptions or predict enzymatic reactions.

## Key Insight
The sequence-structure-function paradigm in biology posits that a protein's biological role is determined by its 3D conformational structure, which is encoded in its primary amino acid sequence. By aligning learned representations from both sequence and structure with language, STELLA captures complementary information: sequence captures evolutionary conservation while structure captures geometric constraints critical for function. The multimodal alignment enables the model to leverage textual knowledge about protein families while grounding predictions in physical molecular properties.
