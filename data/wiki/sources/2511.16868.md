## Core Mechanism
The Joint Gromov-Wasserstein (JGW) objective extends the traditional Gromov-Wasserstein (GW) distance framework to enable simultaneous matching between collections of objects, rather than just pairwise matching between two single objects. The method formulates a non-negative dissimilarity measure that identifies partially isomorphic distributions of mm-spaces (metric measure spaces). It adapts traditional Optimal Transport algorithms, including entropic regularization, to solve the joint matching problem for point cloud representations. The objective function optimizes a joint coupling that aligns multiple source objects to multiple target objects in a single optimization, capturing structural relationships across the entire collection.

## Key Insight
The fundamental limitation of traditional GW is its pairwise nature—it can only match two objects at a time. Many real-world problems require matching multiple objects to a single reference or matching collections to collections. The JGW insight is that by formulating a joint objective that considers all objects simultaneously, the method can leverage shared structural information across the collection, leading to more robust matching. This approach provides theoretical guarantees including point sampling convergence, ensuring that as point cloud resolution increases, the matching solution approaches the true geometric solution.

## Domain-Blind Abstraction
This is a method for simultaneously comparing and aligning multiple collections of weighted point sets (or metric spaces) based on their internal geometric structure. It extends pairwise distance-based matching to group-level matching by optimizing a joint coupling matrix that assigns correspondences across all objects in the collection at once. The method uses entropic regularization to make the optimization tractable and converges reliably as sampling resolution increases.
