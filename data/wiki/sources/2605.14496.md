## Core Mechanism
The method combines NMR relaxation experiments with molecular dynamics (MD) simulations to detect subtle entropy changes in proteins. NMR experiments measure order parameters (S²), which quantify the angular fluctuations of N-H bond vectors in the protein backbone. These order parameters serve as experimental restraints in MD simulations, forcing the simulated structural ensembles to match observed dynamics. By generating multiple conformations consistent with experimental data, the method calculates the conformational entropy of the native state using statistical mechanics relationships between ensemble diversity and entropy.

## Key Insight
The hydrophobic core of proteins is not rigid but exhibits nanosecond-timescale fluctuations that contribute to stability through entropy. Even conservative mutations (substituting phenylalanine with leucine or valine) can measurably alter these fluctuations. The resulting entropy changes correspond to free energy variations of several kcal/mol—meaning native state dynamics contribute significantly to protein stability, not just enthalpic interactions.

## Domain-Blind Abstraction
A method for detecting small changes in molecular flexibility by using experimental measurements of motion as constraints in computational simulations. By generating an ensemble of molecular structures that satisfy experimental observations, one can quantify the "freedom to move" (entropy) of the system. This approach reveals that seemingly minor structural changes can substantially alter the energy landscape through entropy contributions.
