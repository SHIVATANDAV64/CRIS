## Core Mechanism
Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are applied to predict molecular properties by representing molecules as graph structures where atoms correspond to nodes and chemical bonds correspond to edges. The network learns representations through message-passing layers, where each node aggregates information from its neighboring nodes, enabling the model to capture the relational and structural information that determines molecular behavior and properties.

## Key Insight
Molecules are inherently graph-structured data, and their properties emerge from the interactions between atoms and their local chemical environments. By treating molecular structures as graphs, GNNs can capture the topological and structural patterns that traditional fingerprint-based or descriptor-based methods often miss, leading to more accurate property predictions for drug discovery applications.

## Domain-Blind Abstraction
Using neural networks that process structured data with entities and relationships to predict the characteristics of complex systems based on their internal connectivity patterns.

## Cross-Domain Potential
- **Materials Science**: Predicting material properties from crystal structures represented as graphs
- **Social Network Analysis**: Predicting user behavior or community detection based on relationship networks
- **Transportation Networks**: Traffic flow prediction and route optimization using road network graphs
