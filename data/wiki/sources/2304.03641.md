## Core Mechanism
The method (OBCD) employs a block coordinate descent strategy to handle nonsmooth composite optimization problems with orthogonality constraints. In each iteration, it updates k rows (where k ≥ 2) of the solution matrix by globally solving a small nonsmooth optimization problem under orthogonality constraints. The key innovation is that the update scheme is **complete** — row-wise orthogonal updates can reach any feasible point from any feasible initialization. The method maintains feasibility throughout iterations and uses breakpoint search techniques to solve the resulting subproblems.

## Key Insight
The completeness of the update scheme is the central insight: by updating k rows simultaneously (rather than single rows or the entire matrix), the method can navigate the feasible space more effectively. This yields **global block-k stationary points** as limit points, which offer stronger optimality guarantees than standard critical points. Additionally, the O(1/ε) iteration complexity and non-ergodic convergence under KL inequality provide solid theoretical backing.
