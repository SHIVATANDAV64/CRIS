## Core Mechanism
The method augments traditional force matching (which trains CG potentials by matching forces from all-atom reference simulations) with stochastic **Hessian-vector product (HVP) matching**. This instills second-order curvature information into the learned potential without explicitly constructing the full Hessian matrix.

The key technical innovation is a decomposition of the target CG Hessian into two components: (1) a **model-independent projected AA Hessian** that is precomputed once from reference all-atom simulations, and (2) a **model-dependent covariance correction** computed online during training at negligible computational cost. An unbiased stochastic estimator is constructed using random probe vectors to approximate the Hessian-matching objective, avoiding the expensive full Hessian computation.

## Key Insight
Force matching alone only constrains the gradient (first derivative) of the free-energy surface, leaving its curvature completely unconstrained. By matching the Hessian (second derivatives), the method captures the local curvature of the energy landscape, which is critical for accurately modeling slow collective motions and conformational dynamics. The decomposition into model-independent and model-dependent terms makes this computationally tractable.

## Domain-Blind Abstraction
A supervised learning framework that improves learned potential functions by adding second-order derivative matching. Uses a decomposition trick where part of the target is precomputed and fixed, while the model-dependent part is computed cheaply online. Employs stochastic probing with random vectors to estimate the full second-derivative information without explicit matrix construction.
